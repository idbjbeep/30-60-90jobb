Days 1–30

Listen, map, and earn trust

Your job this month isn't to fix anything — it's to understand everything. Ask more questions than you answer. Be visibly curious and reliably fast on small tasks.

SharePoint scoping

Relationship goal
Know 40+ employees by name
Walk the floor. Join lunch. Attend every all-hands. People adopt tools built by someone they trust.

Foundation ▼
  • 🤝Schedule 1-on-1s with HR Business Partner and HR Director in week 1 — ask them: "What would success look like at 90 days?" relationship

  • 👂Shadow your colleagues on 2–3 different task types. Take notes, ask "why is it done this way?" — not to criticise, but to understand. relationship
  • 📝Start a daily log: 3 things you learned, 1 question to follow up, 1 win (however small). 5 minutes at end of day. strategy
🏁Week 1 milestone: All system access confirmed. First 1-on-1s done. Know your team's top 3 priorities.

Discovery ▼
  • 🔍Audit what currently lives in the intranet / SharePoint draft: what content exists, what's outdated, what's missing. sharepoint
  • 🎤Conduct 6–8 short interviews (15 min each) with employees across departments: "Where do you go to find HR info today? What frustrates you?" sharepoint
  • 📐Learn Quinyx and TeamTailor from the inside — run a test cycle in both. Note any workflow gaps you spot. systems

Weeks 3–4 — SharePoint architecture plan & quick wins

  • 📊Present a short summary to the team: "Here's what I've found, here's my proposed plan." This signals strategic thinking — rare at junior level. strategy
  • 🔧Resolve any Quinyx or TeamTailor issues you've spotted. Fix small things fast — it builds your reputation as reliable. systems

Now you build. SharePoint goes live in phases. You run training. You make sure people actually use it — adoption is the real deliverable, not the site itself.

Primary project
SharePoint launch — phase 1
Core pages live, all HR policies accessible, navigation tested with real users. Send comms, run walk-through sessions.
Visibility goal
Be the go-to person
When anyone has a system question — Quinyx, TeamTailor, SharePoint — they think of you first. That's your brand.
Weeks 5–6 — Build core SharePoint site
Building ▼
  • 🖥️Build out the approved SharePoint architecture: HR policies, onboarding info, org chart, key contacts, FAQ. Keep design clean and consistent. sharepoint
  • ✅Run a content review with HR Director before publishing — you want sign-off, not surprises. strategy
  • 🧪User test with 3–4 employees before broad launch: "Can you find the parental leave policy? How long did it take?" Fix friction points. sharepoint

🏁Week 6 milestone: Phase 1 of SharePoint ready to launch. Launch comms drafted and approved.
Weeks 7–8 — Launch, train, and measure
Launch ▼
  • 🚀Launch SharePoint phase 1. Announce in all-hands or via all-staff email. Pin it to Teams as a tab. sharepoint
  • 🎓Run 2–3 short training sessions (20–30 min each) for different employee groups. Record one for async access. sharepoint
  • 📈Set up SharePoint analytics — track page views, most visited pages, search terms. This becomes your adoption evidence. sharepoint
  • 🔄Run a Quinyx audit — are schedules being used correctly? Are there recurring errors? Document and fix. systems
  • 💬Collect feedback via a short 3-question form after training sessions. Use it to improve the site, and reference it as evidence of listening. relationship
🏁Day 60 milestone: SharePoint live and employees trained. Adoption metrics tracked. Feedback collected and acting on it.

Promotion playbook

In a flat Norwegian HR team, promotions don't come from time served — they come from taking ownership, solving problems nobody asked you to solve, and making your impact legible to your manager.

1
Own the SharePoint project end-to-end
Don't just build it — track adoption metrics, run training, collect feedback, iterate. Present a quarterly usage report. This turns a task into a strategic initiative you lead, and it's measurable proof of impact at any performance review.
2
Become the system expert everyone relies on
Being the undisputed go-to for Quinyx, TeamTailor and O365 makes you load-bearing for the team. Document your knowledge in SharePoint (guides, FAQs) — this shows you think about the org, not just your own work. Expertise that only lives in your head is less promotable than expertise that multiplies.
3
Fix one thing that wasn't your job
By day 60, you will have spotted at least one HR process that's inefficient, manual, or broken. Pick the one you can actually fix, propose a solution, and fix it. Solving problems outside your remit is the clearest signal that you're operating above your level.
4
Make your work visible — without bragging
Send a short monthly update to your HR Director: "Here's what shipped, here's adoption, here's what's next." In Norwegian work culture, self-promotion feels awkward — so frame it as keeping the team informed. Your wins log is your source of truth for these updates and for performance reviews.
5
Have the conversation explicitly
At your 90-day review, ask: "What would I need to show over the next 6 months to move into a more senior role?" Norwegian managers rarely volunteer this — but they respect directness. It also anchors the conversation so you know exactly what to work toward, rather than hoping someone notices.
6
Invest in credentials that matter
Consider a Microsoft 365 Certified: Fundamentals cert (directly relevant to your role), or an HR analytics course. It shows initiative, adds formal credibility, and gives you something concrete to bring to a salary review — "I've taken on X, completed Y, and delivered Z." That's a raise conversation.
